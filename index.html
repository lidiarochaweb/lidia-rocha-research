Academic & Professional Profile

Lídia Christine Leite Rocha is a research-driven Psychology scholar distinguished by intellectual rigor, strategic analytical thinking, and a sustained commitment to academic excellence at an international standard. Her work is grounded in a strong scientific orientation, with primary interests in Neuroscience, Hospital Psychology, Suicide Prevention, and Forensic Behavioral Science. Her academic interests concentrate on the neurobiological foundations of emotional regulation, behavioral risk assessment, crisis intervention models, and the psychological dynamics underlying vulnerability and resilience. She is particularly interested in translational research — integrating empirical neuroscience with applied clinical and hospital-based psychological practice. As an Active Listening Volunteer at CVV (Centro de Valorização da Vida), she has developed structured competencies in suicide risk awareness, crisis containment, ethical communication, and evidence-informed emotional support. This experience reinforces her applied understanding of acute psychological distress and strengthens her long-term objective of contributing to preventive mental health frameworks and public health strategies. Her training in competitive academic debate further enhances her profile, reflecting formal development in structured reasoning, high-level argumentation, persuasive communication, and critical evaluation of complex theoretical frameworks — competencies that directly strengthen her scientific writing and research presentation skills. Beyond psychology, she possesses advanced expertise in Graphic Design, with professional proficiency in Adobe Creative Suite and Figma, enabling high-level academic presentation development, research visualization, and scientific communication design. Lídia positions herself as a highly competitive international graduate candidate, motivated by interdisciplinary collaboration, methodological precision, and high-impact research production. Her long-term academic goal is to contribute to global research initiatives in neuropsychology, suicide prevention models, behavioral risk analysis, and hospital-based psychological intervention, generating scholarship that combines scientific innovation with measurable humanitarian impact.
